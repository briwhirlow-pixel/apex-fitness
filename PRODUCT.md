# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Prospective gym members in West Chester, PA — adults 25-45 who take training seriously but want a studio that feels curated, not chaotic. They're searching for a gym that matches their standards: expert coaching, real programming, a space that respects their time. They arrive via Google, Instagram bio link, or word-of-mouth referral. The site's job is to make them book a free trial hour before they close the tab.

Secondary audience: potential BuiltByBrian clients — business owners evaluating Brian's portfolio. They're judging craft, attention to detail, and whether the site feels genuinely hand-built or template-dragged.

## Product Purpose

APEX Performance Studio is a portfolio sample build for BuiltByBrian (builtbybwhirl.com). It demonstrates what a premium fitness studio client receives: a single-page site with six class programs, three coach profiles, three-tier pricing, a free trial pass CTA, and local SEO — all hand-coded in Next.js with GSAP motion and real photography.

Success looks like: a prospective BuiltByBrian client visits this site and says "I want mine to feel like that."

## Brand Personality

Premium. Curated. Sharp.

APEX speaks like a head coach who respects your time — direct, confident, zero filler. The copy is tight and specific ("45 min. 16 spots. Maximum effort.") not aspirational fluff ("start your journey"). The design earns its premium positioning through restraint and precision, not decoration.

## Anti-references

- **Generic AI fitness templates**: dark mode + red accent + stock gym photo + stat banner + numbered steps. The exact pattern every AI tool generates for "fitness website." If it looks like a Vercel template with gym photos swapped in, it's failed.
- **Corporate gym chains** (Planet Fitness, LA Fitness): impersonal, stock-photo heavy, coupon-driven, built for volume not quality.
- **CrossFit box aesthetic**: skull logos, warehouse grit, "embrace the suck" energy. APEX is sharp, not grungy.

## Design Principles

1. **Proof over promise** — show the coaches, the classes, the schedule, the prices. No vague "transform your body" copy. Specifics build trust.
2. **Restraint is premium** — fewer elements, more white space, tighter typography. Every pixel earns its place. If removing it doesn't hurt, it shouldn't be there.
3. **The portfolio test** — every section must make a BuiltByBrian prospect think "this couldn't be a template." Structural variety, intentional asymmetry, details that signal a human made choices.
4. **Photography carries the weight** — real images of real spaces, not decorated text blocks. The design serves the photography, not the other way around.
5. **One clear next action** — every scroll position has one obvious thing to do: book the free trial. The CTA hierarchy never competes with itself.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA compliance. Body text contrast ≥ 4.5:1. Touch targets ≥ 44px. Reduced motion support (already implemented). Keyboard navigation for all interactive elements. Alt text on all images.
